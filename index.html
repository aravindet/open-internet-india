Today, when we pay for an internet plan, we get access to the entire internet — whether it is popular apps and sites like WhatsApp, Facebook and YouTube, or less popular ones like sculptures of pets made of felt. This principle, that internet service providers should provide equal access to all websites, is known as net neutrality.

We might lose this freedom by the end of April. Due to intense lobbying by telecom operators, the Indian Government is considering new rules that would allow operators like Reliance, Vodafone and Airtel to block apps apps and websites that they don’t like. Their aim is to block WhatsApp to force us to go back to SMS, charge us extra for accessing YouTube, or force Flipkart to pay them to remain accessible — a cost that will be passed on to us through higher prices.

If this happens, it would be the end the free, innovative internet that we know today. Popular free services like Facebook would never exist if they had to pay money to every ISP in the world before they could start.

If you want to preserve the future of the open internet in India, please join us to oppose this proposal. Together, let’s remind the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) that their job is to protect the rights of consumers, not the profit margins of telcos. Let's tell them that when we pay for internet access, we expect access to the entire internet, not just the sites and apps that Airtel or Vodafone approve of.

How can I get involved?

1. Please sign the petition.

2. TRAI has published a consultation paper asking for public comments on their proposed regulation by 24 April 2015. Help us formulate a strong response and join us in sending it to them.

3. Help spread awareness. Share this page with your friends on Facebook and Twitter. Talk about it at lunch. If you have a website, please do get in touch. We could work together in raising awareness.
